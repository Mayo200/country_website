Society

Nigeria has a thriving middle-income, mixed economy with considerable growth in the manufacturing, financial, service, communications, technology, and entertainment sectors. It is currently the 31st-largest economy in the world in terms of nominal GDP and the largest in Africa, as well as the 27th-largest when measured by purchasing power parity. Its economy is the biggest in Africa. The country's manufacturing sector has seen resurgence, and by 2013 it had become the largest in the continent. It is responsible for a significant portion of goods and services supplied to the West African region. In 2021, the International Monetary Fund(IMF) reported that Nigeria's debt-to-GDP ratio was 36.63%. The currency of Nigeria is the Nigerian Naira(NGN) . The term Naira, which originated from Chief Obafemi Awolowo, is a play on the country's designation Nigeria. The Nigerian Naira is reissued by the Central Bank of Nigeria, which was set up in 1958 and started reissuing its own currency in 1973. The currency was originally introduced in 1973, replacing the Pound grand at a rate of 2 Naira for 1 pound. In 1985, it encountered its 1st devaluation because of an economic crisis and also has got since been pegged to the United States Dollar at distinct rates for the purpose of administer inflation within Nigeria's economy. The Central Bank of Nigeria problems and copes the currency. One Nigerian Naira is equivalent to 0.0022 US Dollars as of jan 2023. The currency can additionally be purchased, exchanged, and traded in several money markets around Nigeria.
