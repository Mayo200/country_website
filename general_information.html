General Information

Nigeria was ruled by military groups from 1966 to 1979 and from 1983 to 1999. Civil war between the federal regime and the previous Eastern region, Biafra(1967–70) ,ended in Biafra’s surrender afterwards the death by starvation of maybe a million Biafrans. The government’s execution of Ken Saro-Wiwa in 1995 led to international sanctions, and civilian govern was by and by reestablished in 1999 with the election of a president. Ethnic strife formerly held in check by periods of military rule erupted in the beforetime twenty-first century, as did demonstrates over oil fabrication in the Niger delta. Friction additionally enhanced between Muslims and Christians afterwards several of the northern and central states adopted Islamic law. Nigeria is a civil democracy in the sense that there's both a public government and governments of its 36 countries, It utilizes the form of government in which the people hold power, but handpick representatives to exercise and use that power with the administrative power exercised by the chairman. The chairman is the head of state, the head of government, and also the head of the multi-party system. Nigerian politics takes place within a frame of a civil, presidential, representative popular democracy, in which administrative power is exercised by the government. Legislative power is held by the civil government and the two chambers of the council the House of Representatives and the Senate, the legislative branch of Nigeria is responsible for and possesses powers for the expression and timber of laws Together, the chambers make up the law-making body in Nigeria, called the public assembly, which serves as a check on the administrative arm of government. Abuja, located in the Federal Capital Territory, is the designated public capital of Nigeria, established by decree in 1976. While Lagos, the previous capital, remains a prominent commercial and urban center. Nigeria's inception as a modern nation dates back to 1914, when the British combined their possessions of Northern and Southern Nigeria. On October 1, 1960, Nigeria gained independence and adopted a democratic constitution in 1963, while still remaining a member of the Commonwealth.
